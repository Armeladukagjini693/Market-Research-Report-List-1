<p><p>The physical hydrogen storage technology market consists of two primary types: high pressure gaseous hydrogen storage and low temperature liquefied hydrogen storage. High pressure gaseous storage involves compressing hydrogen gas into cylinders at elevated pressures, maximizing space efficiency for transport and use. In contrast, low temperature liquefied hydrogen storage involves cooling hydrogen to below its boiling point, converting it into a liquid state for increased density and storage capacity. Both methods are essential for developing efficient hydrogen fuel systems and supporting energy transition efforts.</p></p>
