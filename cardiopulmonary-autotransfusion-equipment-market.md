<p><p>The cardiopulmonary autotransfusion equipment market is primarily divided into two types: unwashed autotransfusion systems (ATS) and washed ATS. Unwashed ATS involves collecting blood without processing it, allowing for a quicker turnaround but may contain contaminants. Washed ATS, on the other hand, involves a thorough cleansing process to remove cellular debris and plasma, enhancing the quality and safety of the reinfused blood. Both types cater to different surgical needs, emphasizing specific patient safety and recovery protocols.</p></p>
